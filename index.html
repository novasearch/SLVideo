SLVideo: A Sign Language Video Moment Retrieval Framework

ACL 2024
Gonçalo Martins, Afonso Quinaz, João Magalhães, Sofia Cavaco, Carla Viegas,
Universidade NOVA de Lisboa,
Paper arXiv Code

Abstract

Sign Language Recognition has been a more and more studied and developed subject throughout the years to help deaf and hard-of-hearing people in their day-to-day lives. These technologies have an advanced recognition of manual signs, still, most of them lack the recognition of facial expressions, which are also an essential part of Sign Language as they allow the speaker to add expressiveness to their dialogue or even change the meaning of certain manual signs.

SLVideo is a video moment retrieval software for Sign Language videos with a focus on signs where facial expressions have a big role. To develop this system, a collection of five hours of annotated Portuguese Sign Language videos is used as the dataset, where it generates a series of embeddings from the extracted video frames to allow the user to use a text query to search for a specific video segment. The initial results were satisfactory, knowing this is a zero-shot task, and allowed us to take away the necessary conclusions so that we could achieve the desired outcomes and help solve the problem of the lack of facial expression sign recognition in Sign Language.

Besides the video moment retrieval task, this system also includes a Sign Language thesaurus and allows the users to add annotations to the dataset.
